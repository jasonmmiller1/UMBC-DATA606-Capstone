# System & Information Integrity Standard — Rivermark Operations Portal (ROP) (Fictional)

> **SYNTHETIC DEMO ARTIFACT — ACADEMIC USE ONLY**

**Version:** 0.1  
**Effective Date:** 2026-03-29  
**Owner:** Security Operations Lead (Fictional Role)  

---

## 1.0 Purpose
Define flaw remediation, malicious code protection, integrity monitoring, and information handling expectations for Rivermark Operations Portal (ROP) (Fictional).

## 2.0 Scope
Applies to applications, supporting infrastructure, endpoints used for administration, and security-relevant data handling associated with Rivermark Operations Portal (ROP) (Fictional).

## 3.0 Roles and Responsibilities
- Security Operations Lead
- Platform Engineering
- System Administrators
- Vulnerability Management Analyst

## 4.0 Standard Statements
- Security-relevant flaws shall be identified, tracked, prioritized, and remediated.
- Integrity of software, configurations, and critical data shall be monitored where feasible.
- Malicious code protection mechanisms shall be deployed consistent with system architecture.
- Security alerts and advisories shall inform remediation prioritization.
- *Intentional gap:* remediation timelines by severity are not defined.

## 5.0 Procedures
1. Collect flaw and vulnerability information from approved sources.
2. Triage findings by severity, exploitability, and system impact.
3. Assign remediation actions to responsible owners.
4. Test and deploy fixes through approved change processes.
5. Monitor integrity alerts and investigate anomalies.
6. Document accepted residual risk where remediation is deferred.
7. *Intentional gap:* no explicit false-positive review process is defined for integrity alerts.

## 6.0 Records
- Vulnerability findings
- Patch deployment evidence
- Integrity monitoring alerts
- Risk acceptance records

## 7.0 Exceptions
Exceptions require documented approval and POA&M tracking.

## 8.0 Review Cadence
Review annually or upon major change.
